SafeScan

In the process of designing and developing an Android privacy-scanning application called SafeScan under the guidance of Professor Rahul Chatterjee at MadS&P Lab, aiming to provide user awareness of installed applications and privacy implications for victims of Intimate Partner Violence (IPV). Implemented an app-analysis feature that scans and categorizes installed apps based on a block-listed database containing over 5,000 records. Classified installed apps into categories such as dual-use and spyware to inform users of potential privacy risks. Enabled direct links to secure app stores and in-device settings for detailed permission management and risk assessment. Developed a feature that provides users with direct access to Google's account privacy settings. Implemented an ADB feature to allow remote device scanning via Wi-Fi or USB, reducing the risk of alerting potential abusers. Utilized method channels to seamlessly integrate Flutter with native Android functionalities.
